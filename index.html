Expectations and considerations

It’s important for me to know if you are expecting something specific like a problem you see in the mix and know that you would like me to solve, a certain loudness level or something that you think I might try to fix that I shouldn't.
‍
If you've applied bus compression or limiting during the mixing stage, please provide two versions: one approved by all involved parties and one without any dynamic processing. For instance, if a compressor heavily influenced the sound but a limiter was added for loudness, it would be ideal to bypass the limiter while ensuring there's no clipping.

Process

I follow a systematic process for mastering your music. It starts and finishes with a thorough listen-through of all the mixes in the correct order. During the initial session, I take notes without interrupting the music.

When that's done, I address the notes and focus on the specifics of each song. I carefully analyze the dynamics and spectrum and use appropriate analog or digital tools to correct or enhance any areas that need attention. After that, I listen across a variety of playback systems and ensure it meets any required standards.

Once I'm satisfied, I print it to the desired file format and proceed with the final listen-through before delivering the finished product.

What to listen for

When you listen to the mastered tracks, useful things to listen for are that the transitions and gaps between songs are to your liking and that the balances in the mix are good.

I'll make sure nothing is technically wrong and address the previously mentioned aspects either based on my judgment or your specific instructions.
However, some elements come down to personal taste.
